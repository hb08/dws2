Food

As any bunny owner can attest, food is one of the most important things to our furred friends. There food choices can be split into two categories, meals and treats. I've also included a list of toxic foods that you should never let your bunny eat.

Meals

Hay

Bunny Tip

Placing food inside or next to a litterbox can inspire your bunny to use it!

It's kind of gross, but bunnies like to poop while they eat.

Rabbits should have hay available at all times, to free feed off of. While young rabbits should be fed alfalfa hay, adults should be limited to timothy, grass, and oat hay, as alfalfa is high in sugars and can make adults overweight. Chewing constantly on hay will help wear down their continously growing teeth, and help with the digestive system.

Choose hay that smells and looks fresh. If it is brown, moldy, and doesn't smell like cut grass, it probably isn't very good for your bunny. Store hay in a dry place, in a container that allows air to flow to prevent mold.

Water

Your bunny will need fresh water at all times. Hanging water bottles are a good option, but bunnies will drink from a bowl as well. If you have a bowl, putting in an ice cube will help your rabbit to want to drink. Keeping vegetables wet after rinsing them off will also keep your rabbit hydrated through the summer months.

Vegetables

Vegetables are a great way to help your bunny get the vitamins they need. You'll want to watch for pesticides, and always wash vegetables before giving them to the bunny. Feed new vegetables in small amounts to prevent illness or an upset tummy. Some vegetables your bunny may love are:

Basil Bok choy Broccoli leaves
Celery Cilantro Clover
Collard greens Dandelion leaves Romaine or Dark Lettuce
Mint Mustard greens Parsley
Water cress

Pellets

Pellets should be high in fiber, low in protein, and kept fresh. Pellets should be limited as your bunny ages, or they may grow obese or have health problems.

Treats

While bunnies love treats, it's up to the owners to limit their intake to prevent obesity. Breads, crackers, pasta, pretzels, cookies, chips, and cereal should never be fed to bunnies. Make sure to check the packaging on bunny treats, as a lot are high in fat and sugar. Never give chocolate to your bunny! Some favorite bunny treats are below, but remember, small doses are the best with these foods!

Apples Never Apple Seeds! Bannanas Carrot tops
Iceberg lettuce Kale Melons
Pineapple Raspberries Strawberries
