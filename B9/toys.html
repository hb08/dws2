Bunny Toys

Your house rabbit will be inquisitive, active, and playful. The best way to help nurture those wonderful traits is through toys! Some toys can be bought while many owners prefer to make their own. Whatever your choice, ensuring your bunny has plenty of playthings is a great way to ensure they don't 'make their own' with your furniture or belongings. Toys have some additional benefits, such as giving your bunny exercise, mental stimulation, and can even extend their lifespan!

Homemade Toys

No-Time Toys

Bunny Tip

Watch your bunny when you first get them. You'll learn what your rabbit loves, digging, chewing, exploration, or all three!

Once you get to know your bunny, you'll be able to cater your toys to what they'll enjoy the most.

Bunnies are not particularly picky when it comes to toys. Here are a few cheap ideas for easy toys that'll keep your bunny occupied!

  • Paper bags and cardboard boxes. They can crawl inside, scratch, and chew to their hearts content. Having multiple entrances to boxes can make them even more fun.
  • Cardboard concrete forms to burrow in.
  • Cardboard rolls from paper towels or toilet paper.
  • Untreated wicker baskets or boxes filled with shredded paper, junk mail, magazines, straw, or (pesticide free) dirt they can dig in.
  • Yellow pages to shred.
  • Rubber balls or Quaker Oat round containers to nudge and roll around. For an extra treat, cut holes in the side of the Quaker box that treats can fall out of.
  • Dried out pinecones to destroy.
  • Things to jump on and off.
  • The hard plastic caps from laundry detergent and softener bottles. Wash off any chemical residue, and they'll love to toss them and dig at them. Never use tops from caustic materials such as drain uncloggers, bleach cleaners, etc, as no amount of washing will make them safe.

A Little Time

Bunny Tip

Switching toys out on a regular basis will keep your bunny more interested in the toys they already have.

Once you have a good grouping of toys, start rotating them in and out.

Here are a couple ideas to help you get your imagination flowing for toys to make for your bunny!

Bunny Maze

Bunny mazes are easy to make, especially if you are as prolific a coke drinker as I am. Keep the boxes the cans come in. You can make them with any cardboard boxes, really. By cutting two or more entrances to each box and securing them together, you can create your own maze.
