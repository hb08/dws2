Associated Press

A young brunette appeared before the recently bombed Seattle headquarters of Peace with Powers, a small group of meta humans that preach non-violence. "The Guild of Killers issued a statement regarding the capture of several of their members by the Minor League last month, purportedly issued by the group's enigmatic leader, Wound-Maker. There is no place for you in this new world so come, let us usher you into the next.' No response has been made by the metahuman community. It leads this reporter to wonder, where is the Freedom League when we need them most?

The three villains who participated in the attack on metahuman Thomas Higgens, known to the public as Bear-Knuckles, Recall, and Elite, as well as the unnamed lackeys that worked in conjunction with them, have all been remanded to federal custody, and will be held at Blackstone until it's decided whether they'll face a normal trial or a military tribunal, since they were acting as members of a terrorist organization. If not for the intercession of the so-called 'Junior League', things could have been far worse, something acknowledged by the police and a number of groups who are currently voicing the rising tide of pro-meta sentiment in Freedom City, despite the rise of anti-metaism elsewheres.

Freedom Ledger

Freedom Ledger Online Forum comments were almost universally supportive, from "I wish you'd saved more for us!" by fozziebear, to, "She deserves a medal, not any kind of charges. She's one of the Lord's special angels," from moarchrist4u.

Christian Science Monitor

The notorious anti-meta blogger and activist James Castle, who somehow managed to acquire inside information on the identities of a number of different metahuman vigilantes, was rushed to the Freedom Hospital West Campus, where he remains in the intensive care unit after suffering burns that cover almost fifty percent of his body. He was found inside of his garage, and the act appeared to be self-inflicted, as there was no evidence of any other sort of assault on Mr. Castle, nor was there any means of accessing the locked garage, although the FCPD said they are still investigating potential leads. Reactions to pro-meta legislation among the hate group's membership have become more violent recently, with two members of the Boise, Idaho branch facing charges of dragging a nineteen year old mutant girl with phosphorescent skin behind a truck and calling it 'glowstick pulling'.

USA Today

What Houston city officials were thinking allowing both events to be planned at the same time remains a mystery. Among the dead was 15 year old Jennifer Richards, whose own meta-powers seemed to have been triggered at the event. Jennifer was marching with Meta NO! when she started glowing violet and beams of energy shot from her fingertips. She was attacked by her fellow marchers and died as she tried to flee.

Freedom Ledger

Tragedy struck in Grenville today. A family of five was discovered dead in their home. Police believe it to be murder suicide, with strong evidence pointing to Thomas De Moion, patriarch of the family, as the culprit. Neighbors are stunned. "They were going on vacation. I think Jerusalem or something, since they said they were celebrating the holiday," one neighbor said. "We thought they were gone." The house had seemed empty from the outside since September 25th, but the family, Thomas, his son Daniel and wife Chaya, along with their twin six-year-old daughters, Nava and Tova, were being held hostage by an increasingly violent Thomas. "The house was boarded up from the inside," one FCPD investigator revealed, "They were trapped in the basement. We have reason to believe Thomas and Daniel were working in concert to keep Chaya, Nava, and Tova hidden in the basement." The sound of screams from the seemingly empty home alerted neighbor and good Samaritan, Dejan LaBelle, that something wasn't right. "I went to check on the house, and when I reached the front door, the screaming had stopped. I pounded on it, and shouted for my wife to call 911. All I could think about were those little girls. I wanted to leave, but I couldn't. I kept thinking if those girls were the ones screaming... so I went further in, the door to the basement was open and... oh man. I really wish I hadn't gone down there. I just... I wish I hadn't at all.

Freedom Ledger

At least 23 students were injured, with 14 in critical care, in a violent spree on Freedom College Campus today. Accounting student, Kenneth Allen, allegedly began attacking students in his Business and Professional Ethics for Accountants class. "He just went crazy," a fellow student, who witnessed the violence, claims. "We were listening to the professor in lecture, and the screaming started. I didn't know what was going on." Allen, according to other eyewitness reports, recieved no provocation to the attack. Utilizing impromptu weapons, the Freedom College student went on a rampage that escalated in violence until the teaching assistant and Freedom City native, Christopher Beck, managed to subdue him. While Beck has yet to make a statement, Natasha Harding was quick to speak on his bravery. "Kenneth was wild. He stabbed Robbie in the neck with a pen, then attacked Professor Fishman with a pair of scissors from his desk. He stabbed him over and over again, and ran out of the room. Chris, he was really calm the whole time. He told Bruce to call 911, then got Stanley and Debra to help the people already hurt. I don't know how he remembered, but Stanley's an Eagle Scout, and Debra is a volunteer firefighter, so he knew they'd be able to. Then he told me to lock the door, that he was going after Kenneth to stop him before he hurt anyone else or himself." By the time Beck was able to stop Kenneth's rampage down the halls, 21 more people had been injured. Witnesses claim Beck spoke to Allen, distracting the crazed boy before tackling him to the ground and pinning him in a high school wrestling move known as 'Double Wrist Lock' until police arrived to the scene and subdue the suspect.

CNN

In light of the escape of Elite, second in line to the Guild of Killers, Senator Edward Bergen has begun advocating the until now ignored legislation S.3124. The proposed legislation, called Justice For All by proponents of the bill, calls for action against 'Metahumans and Metahuman Affiliates'. This law, if passed, will declare all of those classified under those terms exempt from trial by jury, to be held in a metahuman correctional facility from arrest through trial, and expedited trials.

Moonbeams

About fifty miles upstream from the river that fueled the main water supply of Freedom City, a small and pearlescent nodule, once buried beneath enough layers of dirt the weight alone should have long ago crushed such a seemingly delicate object, wriggled, the journey's end finally arriving, as it pushed through the surface. As it finally ceased it's perpetual upward motion, however slow, the nodule waited...and waited... and waited.

When the small biological clock deep inside began to ring, it burst open on a night where the silver rays of the moon, diffused into an equally silver glow beneath the water's surface, caught the sparkling mote cloud of seeds from the now empty shell. Carried with the current, the motes disappeared from sight as the only cloud in the sky covered the moon.

Moonlit McMansions

It was obviously nighttime, judging by the pale radiance that shone down and nearly illuminated the entirety of the strange suburban block. It looked like a portion of Freedom City, but unfamiliar, as if it's features had shifted enough to be nearly unrecognizable. Three massive manor houses stood before him, but the whole of the block that he was on was eerily silent, not even the buzzing of insects or the noise of animals present.

The first residence had it's palatial front doors wide open, and as he stepped inside, there were signs of a struggle in the foyer with a vase shattered, it's wet contents scattered about the floor. A trail of dried bloodstains led into a sitting room, but when he got inside, there was nothing there; no pool of blood, no furniture, no decorations. Only the large square panes of soft light from the outside. The whole house was much the same, and as he exited through the door to the backyard, he suddenly found himself at the next house down the block, it's front bathed in the same omnipresent light.

The other two houses on the block had been similarly sinister; abandoned, down to the absence of mouse droppings or spiderwebs, it was as if their inhabitants had suddenly disappeared, vanishing into thin air, never to return. When he'd left the third and final building, he found himself on the blacktop of the street between them, walking towards the end of the block as the night was lit nearly as bright as day, the moon's rays almost like sunlight, causing him to squint. It was what he saw as he passed the mailboxes that brought his heart to a near still. The names Driogano, Tonifanni, and Oliverti.

Then, there was sunlight, as the Venetian blinds in the office room failed to block out the day's angry rays. The repeating tone of an instant messenger's typing field that had seen it's end reached chimed as blond, long hair was pushed out of the way of a pair of eyes that immediately looked around for a telephone. He wasn't certain what it meant, but it couldn't be good.

Maudlin Moonlit Machinations

Jacob Miller was a powerful man. 6'4" and 210lbs of muscles. Able to keep pace with even the fittest and newest of recruits from the academy, even at 46. He held the highest-ranking position of anyone in law enforcement in the city. Yet, while he sat behind his desk, in the corner office so many men were striving to reach, he felt completely powerless. His daughter was missing. The entire police force, adoptive family to the girl, were searching for her. Criminals had been tapped from the crime lords, to the mob bosses, down to petty thieves and hookers. No one had seen her. No one could find her.

The door to his office cracked open, and his head snapped up, the lines in his face not there the morning before a dark roadmap of his unhappiness. His features lightened. "Did they- What are you doing here?" Concern hit his voice, and his expression was startled as he spotted the glint of metal in a familiar hand. "Wh-" any further questions were cut off as the gunshot echoed through his office and the station. His hand went up to his chest before he registered what had happened. His ears were ringing, and warmth was bubbling over his hand, thick and hot. He looked down at the blood pouring out of the bullet wound.

Footsteps pounded down the hallway, too far away to stop this. Too far away to help. The shadows of the room were closing in around him, and he stared up at the moon through his window before his eyes finally closed.
